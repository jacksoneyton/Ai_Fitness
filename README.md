1. Enhance muscle growth and aesthetics.
2. Increase strength with functional application.
3. Introduce foundational speed training.
4. Improve flexibility and mobility.

## **Additional Recommendations**

### **Asthma and Circulation**
- Warm up thoroughly with light cardio and dynamic stretching before workouts to improve circulation and manage asthma.

### **Sleep Improvement**
- Consider pre-sleep magnesium supplementation and reducing screen time 2 hours before bed.

### **Caloric Intake for Weight Maintenance/Gain**
- Target 300-500 kcal surplus with a focus on:
  - Protein: 1g/lb body weight.
  - Healthy fats.
- Prioritize post-workout meals rich in protein and carbs (e.g., chicken, sweet potato).

### **Recovery Practices**
- Spend 15 minutes daily on foam rolling, stretching, or yoga.
- Maintain electrolyte balance post-workout with hydration and light sodium intake.

# Expanded Stretches and Mobility Drills

---

## **1. Shoulder and Thoracic Spine Stretches**

**Purpose**: Improve upper body flexibility, reduce tension in the shoulders and upper back, and enhance thoracic spine mobility for better posture and lifting performance.

### **Examples**
1. **Thread the Needle**
   - Kneel on all fours.
   - Slide one arm underneath the other arm and rotate your torso.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds on each side.

2. **Doorway Stretch**
   - Stand in a doorway with your arms bent at 90 degrees and hands resting on the frame.
   - Step forward with one foot, gently pressing your chest through the doorway.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds.

3. **Cat-Cow Stretch**
   - Start on all fours.
   - Inhale, arch your back and look upward (Cow).
   - Exhale, round your back and tuck your chin (Cat).
   - Repeat 8-10 times for dynamic mobility.

4. **Foam Rolling for Thoracic Spine**
   - Place a foam roller under your upper back.
   - Cross your arms over your chest and gently roll up and down the spine.
   - Pause on tight spots for 20-30 seconds.

---

## **2. Hip Flexor and Hamstring Stretches**

**Purpose**: Relieve tightness in the hip flexors and hamstrings, improve hip mobility, and reduce lower back strain.

### **Examples**
1. **Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch**
   - Kneel on one knee with the other foot forward in a lunge position.
   - Push your hips slightly forward while keeping your chest upright.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds on each side.

2. **Standing Hamstring Stretch**
   - Stand and place one foot on an elevated surface (e.g., a bench or low table).
   - Keep your leg straight and hinge at your hips to reach forward.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds on each leg.

3. **Seated Forward Fold**
   - Sit on the floor with legs extended forward.
   - Hinge at your hips and reach for your toes, keeping your back flat.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds.

4. **World’s Greatest Stretch**
   - Start in a plank position.
   - Step one foot forward next to your hand.
   - Rotate the same-side arm upward, then return and switch sides.

---

## **3. Shoulder Mobility Drills and Stretching**

**Purpose**: Enhance the range of motion and reduce stiffness in the shoulders for better overhead lifting and injury prevention.

### **Examples**
1. **PVC Shoulder Pass-Throughs**
   - Hold a PVC pipe or broomstick with a wide grip.
   - Lift it over your head and behind your back in a slow, controlled motion.
   - Repeat 8-10 times.

2. **Wall Angels**
   - Stand with your back flat against a wall, arms bent at 90 degrees.
   - Slowly slide your arms upward like making a "snow angel."
   - Keep your back and arms in contact with the wall throughout.

3. **Scapular Retractions**
   - Lie face-down on a bench.
   - Extend your arms forward and squeeze your shoulder blades together while pulling your arms back.
   - Hold for 2-3 seconds and release.

4. **Sleeper Stretch**
   - Lie on your side with your bottom arm bent at 90 degrees.
   - Use your top hand to gently press your bottom hand toward the floor.
   - Hold for 20-30 seconds.

---

## **4. Dynamic Stretches and PNF for Hips, Quads, and Hamstrings**

**Purpose**: Prepare the muscles for activity (dynamic stretches) and improve flexibility and muscle function through active engagement (PNF).

### **Dynamic Stretches**
1. **Leg Swings**
   - Hold onto a sturdy object for balance.
   - Swing one leg forward and backward in a controlled motion.
   - Perform 10 swings per leg.

2. **Walking Lunges with a Twist**
   - Step forward into a lunge and twist your torso toward the front leg.
   - Alternate legs as you walk forward.
   - Repeat 8-10 times per leg.

3. **High Knees**
   - Jog in place, bringing your knees to your chest with each step.
   - Perform for 30-60 seconds.

4. **Toy Soldiers**
   - Walk forward while kicking your legs straight up to touch your opposite hand.
   - Alternate legs for 10-12 steps.

### **PNF Stretches**
1. **PNF Hamstring Stretch**
   - Lie on your back with one leg extended upward.
   - Use a strap or towel to pull the leg gently until you feel resistance.
   - Contract the hamstring by pressing against the strap for 5-10 seconds.
   - Relax and pull the leg deeper into the stretch for another 10-15 seconds.

2. **PNF Quad Stretch**
   - Stand on one leg and hold the opposite ankle behind you.
   - Pull the ankle gently while contracting the quad against your hand for 5-10 seconds.
   - Relax and deepen the stretch for 10-15 seconds.

3. **PNF Hip Flexor Stretch**
   - Kneel in a lunge position with one knee on the floor.
   - Push your hips forward slightly and press the back leg into the floor.
   - Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then relax and deepen the stretch.

4. **PNF Butterfly Stretch**
   - Sit with your feet together and knees bent outward.
   - Gently press your knees toward the floor using your elbows while contracting your inner thighs.
   - Hold the contraction for 5-10 seconds, then deepen the stretch.
